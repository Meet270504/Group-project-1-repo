Welcome to the Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon is one of the most famous natural landmarks in the world, known for its immense size and its intricate and colorful landscape.

Geographical Facts

Immense Size

The Grand Canyon stretches 277 miles in length, it’s 18 miles wide and plunges over a mile deep in some areas, showcasing Earth's grandeur at its most breathtaking.

Sculpted by the Colorado River

This masterpiece was sculpted over millions of years by the relentless power of the Colorado River. Imagine water cutting through rock to reveal the Earth's hidden layers.

Ancient Rock Layers

The rock layers in the Grand Canyon date back nearly 2 billion years, offering an unprecedented glimpse into Earth's ancient history.

Historical Significance

U.S. National Park

In 1919, the Grand Canyon was designated a U.S. National Park, protecting its natural beauty for generations to come. It now stands as a symbol of America’s natural heritage.

Native American Heritage

For thousands of years, Native American tribes, such as the Havasupai and Hopi, called the canyon home. Their history is embedded in the cliffs, trails, and stories of this majestic land.

Visitor Experience

Open Year-Round

Open year-round on the South Rim and seasonally on the North Rim, the Grand Canyon invites visitors to explore its vast beauty through hiking, rafting, and helicopter tours, making it one of the most visited natural landmarks in the world.

Helicopter Tours

A helicopter ride over the canyon offers an unmatched bird’s-eye view, allowing visitors to fully appreciate the sheer scale of this natural wonder.
